## For Children

### Getting Started

1. **Open the App**
   - Go to http://localhost:3000
   - You'll see a colorful welcome screen

2. **Select Your Profile**
   - If you've used the app before, click your name
   - If you're new, click "New Session" and enter:
     - Your name
     - Your age
     - Select boy/girl/neutral theme

3. **Choose an Activity**
   - Story Time - Listen to AI-generated stories
   - Homework Helper - Get help with schoolwork
   - I Spy Game - Play visual games
   - Free Chat - Talk about anything

4. **Start Talking!**
   - Type messages in the chat box
   - Or click the microphone to speak
   - The AI will respond with helpful, friendly answers

---

### Activity Modes

#### Story Time

**What it does:** Creates custom stories just for you!

**How to use:**
1. Select "Story Time" from the activity menu
2. Tell the AI what kind of story you want:
   - "Tell me a story about dinosaurs"
   - "I want an adventure story"
   - "Tell me a princess story"
3. The AI will generate a unique story
4. Click the speaker icon to hear it read aloud

**Tips:**
- Stories are age-appropriate based on your profile
- You can ask for short, medium, or long stories
- Request specific themes (animals, space, magic, etc.)

---

#### Free Chat

**What it does:** Talk about anything on your mind!

**How to use:**
1. Select "Free Chat" from the activity menu
2. Ask questions or have a conversation
3. The AI can:
   - Answer questions about science, history, animals, etc.
   - Play word games
   - Tell jokes
   - Give advice (school, friends, hobbies)
   - Teach you new things

**Examples:**
- "Why is the sky blue?"
- "Tell me a funny joke"
- "How do birds fly?"
- "I'm feeling bored, what can I do?"

**Safety:** The AI monitors conversations and will alert your parents if you mention something concerning.

---

#### Homework Helper

**What it does:** Helps you understand your homework (without giving you all the answers!)

**How to use:**
1. Select "Homework Helper"
2. Click the camera button
3. Take a picture of your homework
4. Ask questions about it

**What it can help with:**
- Math problems (explaining steps)
- Reading comprehension questions
- Science concepts
- Writing assignments (brainstorming, editing)

**Important:** The AI won't just give you answers - it will guide you to figure them out yourself!

**Tips:**
- Take clear pictures with good lighting
- Ask specific questions
- If you don't understand, ask the AI to explain differently

---

#### I Spy Game

**What it does:** Play "I Spy" using your camera!

**How to use:**
1. Select "I Spy Game"
2. Click the camera button
3. Take a picture of your surroundings
4. The AI will:
   - Identify objects in the picture
   - Give you clues to find things
   - Ask you questions about what you see

**Examples:**
- "I spy something red in the picture"
- "Can you find the teddy bear?"
- "How many books are in the picture?"

**Tips:**
- Play in different rooms for variety
- Use good lighting for better recognition
- Try to stump the AI by taking tricky pictures!

---

### Using the Camera

**When you need it:**
- Homework Helper (show your worksheet)
- I Spy Game (show your room)
- Show and Tell (share something cool)

**How to use:**
1. Click the camera button (looks like a camera icon)
2. Allow camera access when your browser asks
3. Position your item/homework in the frame
4. Click "Take Picture"
5. The AI will analyze it and respond

**Privacy:**
- The camera only activates when YOU click the button
- Pictures are only sent when you click "Take Picture"
- Your parents can see when you use the camera

---

### Voice Features

**Two ways to use voice:**

#### 1. Speech-to-Text (Speaking to the AI)

1. Click the microphone button
2. Speak clearly
3. Your words appear as text
4. The AI responds

**Tips:**
- Speak clearly and not too fast
- Reduce background noise
- If it misunderstands, type your message instead

#### 2. Text-to-Speech (AI speaks to you)

1. When the AI responds, click the speaker icon
2. Listen to the AI read the message aloud

**Great for:**
- Story Time (immersive storytelling)
- When you're tired of reading
- Learning pronunciation

---

### Emergency Button

**What it is:** A big red button that calls for help

**When to use it:**
- You're hurt
- You're scared
- Something dangerous happens
- You need an adult RIGHT NOW

**What happens:**
1. Click the red "Emergency" button
2. Your parent gets an instant alert
3. You can call your emergency contact
4. Help is on the way!

**Important:** Only use for real emergencies, not for asking questions or getting attention.

## For Parents

### Accessing the Dashboard

1. **Login**
   - Go to http://localhost:3000/parent-login
   - Enter your credentials:
     - Email: `parent@example.com`
     - Password: `parent123`

2. **Dashboard Overview**

   You'll see a tab-based interface with:
   - **Children** - Manage child profiles
   - **Alerts** - View safety notifications
   - **Activities** - See what your children are doing
   - **AI Assistant** - Get parenting advice
   - **Sources** - View AI response citations
   - **Settings** - Configure preferences

---

### Managing Children

#### Creating Child Profiles

1. Click the **Children** tab
2. Click **"Add Child"**
3. Fill in:
   - Name
   - Age
   - Gender (or neutral)
   - Emergency contact phone number
4. Click **"Save"**

#### Auto-Discovery

If your children have already used the app:

1. Click **"Auto-Discover Children"**
2. The system scans past sessions
3. Creates profiles for unique children
4. You can edit profiles afterward

#### Editing Profiles

1. Click on a child's card
2. Click **"Edit"**
3. Update information
4. Click **"Save Changes"**

#### Profile Pictures

1. Edit a child's profile
2. Click **"Upload Profile Picture"**
3. Select an image from your computer
4. Picture is displayed on the profile card

#### Child Settings

Click **"Settings"** on a child's card to configure:

**Allowed Activities:**
- Story Time
- Homework Helper
- I Spy Game
- Free Chat

**Content Filter Level:**
- Strict (ages 3-7)
- Moderate (ages 8-12)
- Relaxed (ages 13+)

**Session Settings:**
- Timeout duration (auto-end inactive sessions)
- Quiet hours (restrict usage times)

**Permissions:**
- Enable/disable camera
- Enable/disable microphone

### Monitoring Conversations

#### Real-Time Monitoring

1. Select a child from the **Children** tab
2. If they have an active session, you'll see:
   - Current activity
   - Real-time message feed
   - Time elapsed

#### Conversation History

1. Click **"View Sessions"** on a child's card
2. See all past sessions with:
   - Date and time
   - Duration
   - Number of messages
   - Activities performed

3. Click a session to view full conversation transcript

#### What You Can See

- All messages between child and AI
- Timestamps
- Activities performed (games, stories, homework)
- Images captured (if any)
- AI reasoning (what the AI was "thinking")
- Source citations for factual responses

### Safety Alerts

#### Alert Levels

**INFO (Blue)**
- Informational only
- No action needed
- Example: "Child started a new activity"

**WARNING (Yellow)**
- Minor concern
- Monitor situation
- Example: "Child mentioned feeling sad"

**URGENT (Orange)**
- Requires attention
- Check on child soon
- Example: "Child mentioned getting hurt"

**EMERGENCY (Red)**
- Immediate action required
- Child pressed panic button
- Example: "Emergency button activated"

#### Viewing Alerts

1. Go to **Alerts** tab
2. See all alerts sorted by severity
3. Click an alert to see:
   - Full context
   - AI assessment
   - Conversation excerpt
   - Recommended actions

#### Resolving Alerts

1. Address the concern with your child
2. Click **"Resolve"** on the alert
3. Optionally add notes for your records

#### Real-Time Notifications

- You'll see a notification badge when new alerts arrive
- Emergency alerts trigger browser notifications (if enabled)
- WebSocket keeps you updated in real-time

### Getting AI Advice

The **AI Assistant** tab provides parenting support powered by NVIDIA AI.

#### Asking Questions

1. Go to **AI Assistant** tab
2. Type your question or concern
3. The AI analyzes and provides:
   - Thoughtful advice
   - Age-appropriate strategies
   - Suggested actions
   - Citations from trusted sources (CDC, AAP, etc.)

#### Example Questions

- "How do I handle tantrums with a 4-year-old?"
- "My child is having trouble with homework, what should I do?"
- "Is it normal for an 8-year-old to be afraid of the dark?"
- "How much screen time is appropriate for a 10-year-old?"

#### Context-Aware Advice

The AI can reference:
- Your child's age and profile information
- Recent conversation themes (if you grant access)
- General parenting best practices

#### Important Notes

- AI **does not** read your child's specific conversations (privacy)
- AI **can** see general themes and concerns from alerts
- Advice is informational, not a replacement for professional help
- For medical/legal issues, always consult professionals

#### Conversation History

- All your questions and AI responses are saved
- Access past advice anytime
- Track what strategies you've tried

---

### Viewing Sources

The **Sources** tab shows citations for AI responses.

#### Why This Matters

- Transparency: See where information comes from
- Trust: Verify AI used reliable sources
- Education: Learn about child development resources

#### What You'll See

For each citation:
- **Source Type:** CDC, CPSC, NIH, AAP, etc.
- **Title:** Document or article name
- **URL:** Link to original source
- **Excerpt:** Relevant text that informed the AI's response
- **Confidence Score:** How relevant it was (0-100)
- **Public Domain Status:** All sources are public domain

#### Filtering Citations

- Filter by session
- Filter by source type
- Sort by relevance or date
- Search by keyword

#### Using Citations

- Click the URL to read the full source
- Verify information independently
- Share with other caregivers
- Use for further research

### Monitoring Children

#### What You Can Do

**View:**
- All children assigned to you
- Active sessions and conversations
- Safety alerts
- Activity logs
- AI response sources

**Monitor:**
- Real-time conversation feed
- Safety status
- Current activities

#### What You Cannot Do

- Create, edit, or delete child profiles
- Change child settings
- Resolve alerts (parent-only)
- Access parent's AI assistant conversations

#### Best Practices

1. **Regular Check-ins**
   - Review alerts tab every 15-30 minutes
   - Monitor active sessions
   - Respond to URGENT or EMERGENCY alerts immediately

2. **Communication**
   - Inform parents of any concerning alerts
   - Keep notes on daily activities
   - Document any incidents

3. **Emergency Response**
   - Know emergency contact numbers
   - Have emergency protocol ready
   - Call parent immediately for URGENT/EMERGENCY alerts

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can the AI see my child through the camera constantly?

**No!** The camera only activates when your child clicks the camera button. We never record or stream video continuously.

### What happens to conversation data?

Conversations are stored in the local database for parent review. We don't sell or share data with third parties.

### Can I delete conversation history?

Yes, you can delete sessions and messages from the parent dashboard settings.

### Is the AI safe for young children?

Yes! The AI:
- Provides age-appropriate responses
- Filters inappropriate content
- Monitors for safety concerns
- Alerts parents to issues
- Uses trusted sources (CDC, CPSC, NIH)

### What if the AI gives wrong information?

The AI uses RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) to provide factual responses from trusted sources. However:
- Always verify critical information
- Check citations in the Sources tab
- Use professional judgment for important decisions

### Can multiple children use the app at once?

Yes! Each child should:
- Have their own profile
- Start their own session
- Use the app on separate devices

The parent dashboard tracks all children simultaneously.
